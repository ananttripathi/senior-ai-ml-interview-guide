# Mock Interview Checklist

Use this before and after every mock interview. The feedback loop is the most important part.

---

## Before the Mock

- [ ] Confirm the round type (ML fundamentals, system design, coding, behavioral)
- [ ] Tell your mock interviewer the company and role you're targeting
- [ ] Set a timer — stick to actual interview duration (45 or 60 min)
- [ ] Have paper/whiteboard ready (or use excalidraw.com for virtual)
- [ ] Close all tabs except a code editor if it's a coding round
- [ ] Record the session (with permission) for self-review

---

## During: What Interviewers Are Watching

### All rounds
- [ ] Do you ask clarifying questions before diving in?
- [ ] Do you think out loud (share your reasoning, not just your answer)?
- [ ] Do you structure your answer before speaking?
- [ ] Do you recover gracefully from mistakes?

### ML Fundamentals
- [ ] Can you explain concepts without jargon first, then go deeper?
- [ ] Do you know the math behind what you're explaining?
- [ ] Can you give real-world examples?
- [ ] Do you acknowledge trade-offs?

### ML System Design
- [ ] Did you clarify scope, constraints, and metrics before designing?
- [ ] Did you cover the full pipeline (data → training → serving → monitoring)?
- [ ] Did you discuss trade-offs for key decisions?
- [ ] Did you connect technical choices back to business goals?
- [ ] Did you manage time (didn't spend 30 min on data, rushed the rest)?

### Coding
- [ ] Did you explain your approach before coding?
- [ ] Did you write clean, readable code (not just correct)?
- [ ] Did you test with examples (including edge cases)?
- [ ] Did you analyze time and space complexity?

### Behavioral
- [ ] Did you use STAR format?
- [ ] Did you say "I" not "we"?
- [ ] Did you have a quantified result?
- [ ] Was the story under 3 minutes?

## Scoring Rubric (Use After Each Round)

Rate yourself 1–4:
- **4** — Strong hire signal. Excellent answer, clear structure, handled follow-ups well.
- **3** — Hire signal. Good answer with minor gaps. Recovered from initial stumble.
- **2** — Weak signal. Incomplete answer, needed significant hints, or didn't handle follow-ups.
- **1** — No hire signal. Couldn't answer the question or answer was fundamentally wrong.

## Recommended Mock Interview Platforms

### Peer Mock (Best Quality)
- Find a study partner at the same level (LinkedIn, Blind, Reddit r/cscareerquestions)
- Swap roles — you interview them, they interview you
- Best when your partner is also targeting MAANG at senior level

### Paid Platforms
- **Interviewing.io** — anonymous mocks with FAANG engineers; you can see their ratings
- **Exponent** — ML/DS-specific mocks, good behavioral and ML design practice
- **Pramp** — free peer-to-peer, mostly coding

### Self-Practice
- Record yourself answering questions solo (camera on)
- Watch back at 1.5x speed — you'll immediately notice filler words, unclear structure
- Review after 24 hours (you'll be more objective)

## Common Failure Modes at Senior Level

### "Too junior" signals
- Waiting for the interviewer to guide you ("what should I focus on next?")
- Designing for a small-scale system without considering production concerns
- Behavioral stories with small individual scope (no cross-team impact)
- Coding: working solution but O(n²) when O(n log n) exists

### "Can't operate in ambiguity" signals
- Asking for all requirements upfront before starting to think
- Getting stuck when the interviewer says "you can make assumptions"
- Not acknowledging trade-offs or presenting only one approach

### "Doesn't communicate well" signals
- Silence for >2 minutes without narrating your thought process
- Answer changes completely when interviewer asks a follow-up (no backbone)
- Can't simplify a complex technical concept for a non-technical audience

## Mock Schedule Template

8 mocks over 4 weeks:

| Week | Mock 1 | Mock 2 |
|------|--------|--------|
| Week 1 | ML fundamentals | Behavioral |
| Week 2 | ML system design | Coding |
| Week 3 | Full loop (2 rounds back-to-back) | Full loop (2 rounds back-to-back) |
| Week 4 | Company-specific (targeted) | Free practice on weakest area |

After Week 4: reflect on patterns in your weaknesses and do targeted practice before real interviews.
