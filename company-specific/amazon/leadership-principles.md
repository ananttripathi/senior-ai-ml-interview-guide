# Amazon Leadership Principles — Interview Guide

Amazon is unique: **every interviewer evaluates Leadership Principles (LPs)**. In a 5-round loop, each interviewer typically owns 2–3 LPs. You will be asked behavioral questions tied to specific LPs in every single round, including technical rounds.

---

## The 16 Leadership Principles

1. Customer Obsession
2. Ownership
3. Invent and Simplify
4. Are Right, A Lot
5. Learn and Be Curious
6. Hire and Develop the Best
7. Insist on the Highest Standards
8. Think Big
9. Bias for Action
10. Frugality
11. Earn Trust
12. Dive Deep
13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
14. Deliver Results
15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility

For senior (L5/L6) roles, expect deeper emphasis on: **Ownership**, **Think Big**, **Dive Deep**, **Deliver Results**, and **Earn Trust**.

---

## How Amazon Interviewers Score You

Interviewers use the STAR method + a "brag sheet" they fill out after. They're looking for:
- Specificity (not vague platitudes)
- YOUR role (not "we")
- Quantified impact
- Reflection (what you'd do differently)

Scale matters at senior level: L5/L6 stories should involve cross-team impact, ambiguity, and influencing without authority.

## LP-by-LP Prep Guide

### Customer Obsession
**Core question**: "Describe a time you put the customer first even when it was difficult."

What distinguishes a great answer:
- A specific customer pain point (not an internal metric you cared about)
- An action that required you to push back on internal pressure or priorities
- Measurable customer outcome

Watch out for: "We improved our NPS score" without specific actions you personally took.

---

### Ownership
**Core question**: "Tell me about a time you took responsibility for something outside your job description."

Great signals:
- You identified a problem no one asked you to fix
- You saw a project through even when it became harder than expected
- You took accountability for a failure without making excuses

Weak signals: stories where you "helped" a project but someone else owned it; or stories where the scope is too small.

---

### Invent and Simplify
**Core question**: "Tell me about a novel solution you developed."

This is NOT just about creativity — "simplify" is equally important. Strong answers:
- Show you found a simpler way to solve a complex problem (e.g., eliminated an entire service, replaced a complex workflow)
- Or show genuine innovation (novel ML approach, new product feature concept)

At senior level: the simplification should have org-wide or product-wide impact.

---

### Are Right, A Lot
**Core question**: "Tell me about a time you used strong judgment when data was limited."

This LP is about quality of judgment and intellectual integrity:
- You sought diverse perspectives before deciding
- You changed your mind based on new evidence (intellectual humility)
- You correctly predicted an outcome others doubted (good judgment)

Don't confuse with "I was right and everyone else was wrong" — humility matters.

---

### Bias for Action
**Core question**: "Tell me about a time you moved quickly without all the information you wanted."

Speed matters at Amazon. Great signals:
- You calculated the risk and made a judgment call
- The action was reversible (or you had a rollback plan)
- Moving fast led to better outcome than waiting

Weak signals: stories where you moved fast and it worked out by luck, with no reasoning shown.

---

### Dive Deep
**Core question**: "Tell me about a time you identified a problem by going beyond the surface."

What they want:
- You didn't accept the first explanation; you investigated further
- You found a root cause others missed
- Specific metrics, logs, or data you dug into

At senior level: this often involves auditing another team's system or process.

---

### Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
**Core question**: "Tell me about a time you pushed back on a decision, even from a senior leader."

Two parts:
1. **Disagree**: you articulated your concern clearly, with data/reasoning, respectfully
2. **Commit**: once the decision was made (even if against your recommendation), you executed fully

Weak: stories where you just complained or passively resisted. Strong: you made your case, it wasn't taken, and you committed fully anyway and supported the decision publicly.

---

### Deliver Results
**Core question**: "Tell me about your most significant professional achievement."

Must have:
- Quantified impact (dollars, %, scale)
- Obstacles you overcame
- YOUR specific role in the outcome
- Timeline

This is often the final LP question in Amazon loops. Have your strongest story ready.

## Amazon-Specific ML Questions

Amazon interviewers often connect LP questions to your technical work. Expect:

**"Tell me about a time you dove deep into a data quality issue."** (Dive Deep + technical)

**"Tell me about an ML model you built that directly impacted customers."** (Customer Obsession + technical)

**"Tell me about a time you simplified a complex ML pipeline."** (Invent and Simplify + technical)

---

## Amazon Interview Loop Structure (Senior MLE/DS)

Typical 5–7 round structure:

| Round | Type | LPs Covered |
|-------|------|-------------|
| Phone screen | ML fundamentals + 1–2 LP questions | Customer Obsession, Deliver Results |
| Onsite 1 | ML/AI system design | Ownership, Think Big |
| Onsite 2 | Coding (LC medium) | Bias for Action |
| Onsite 3 | ML domain deep dive | Dive Deep, Invent & Simplify |
| Onsite 4 | Leadership/cross-team scenarios | Earn Trust, Backbone |
| Onsite 5 | Bar raiser | All LPs, especially the harder ones |
| Onsite 6 (L6+) | VP-level strategy | Think Big, Success & Scale |

**Bar Raiser**: a trained Amazon interviewer (not on the hiring team) who has veto power and evaluates if you raise the overall bar of the team. They often ask harder LP questions and probe follow-ups more deeply.

---

## Day-of Tips

- Every story must use "I", not "we"
- If they say "that's a great example, tell me more about X" — they want to go deeper, not hear a new story
- It's OK to pause for 10–15 seconds to think before answering
- End every story with a "result" that has numbers
- Prepare 2 versions of each story: 2-minute and 4-minute versions
